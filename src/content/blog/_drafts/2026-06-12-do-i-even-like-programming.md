For an incredibly long time, I've had this core tension in my head of really wanting to build apps and stuff, but not knowing what to build. It feels like a race I am losing. Every moment I could be building, but am not because I don't know what to build, feels like a waste and like I have failed. Lately, I've just been feeling down about it all. I really enjoy building apps, especially iOS apps, but [Apple rejected an app](https://samwarnick.com/blog/to-native-or-not-part-ii/) and I just got real grumpy and sour about all of it. I shifted to focusing on the web, but I think building web apps just does not have the same sense of satisfaction for me. It requires a lot more decision making, which is not my strongest feature. I started feeling wound up—feeling frustrated about not knowing what to build, web vs native, not knowing if I can or should use AI, what stack to build with, and more. Ugh.

Dave said it's okay to take a break, so I'm taking a break.

At least for 30 days. I just need a good clean reset. Need some time to get some perspective and clarity. I'm allowing myself to think about apps and write down ideas, but I'm not going to look at or touch code[^1]. I'm going to try and focus on some other hobbies. I enjoy drawing and would like to make it a habit and actually improve. I started playing Hades 2 on the Switch 2. I'm terrible at it. Like truly terrible. Even with God Mode on. But I'm enjoying it. Something about doing the same thing over and over is nice. I bought an embroidery kit. Not sure if it's for me yet.

To be honest, it's been like 3 days and I already feel the itch build something. Probably because WWDC is still fresh. I fully expect to come back to programming and building. Deep down, I really do enjoy it. But I want to take time to really understand what I want. I've been making a list of people who inspire me and are doing what I would like to do[^2]. I've been pretty aimless the last couple of years and I would like to change that.

[^1]: Except for like if my homelab crashes down or something.
[^2]: I'll share that at some point.
