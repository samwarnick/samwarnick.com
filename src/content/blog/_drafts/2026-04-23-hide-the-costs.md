title: Hide the Costs

---

As I was driving home from taking my kids to school, I thought about how I do not know how much it actually costs me drive them every day, and I don't want to know[^1].

I don't like thinking too much about how every little thing actually costs me. I tend to prefer things wrap costs together and hide them from me. I paid for a maintenance plan on our last car so that I don't have to think about things like tires and oil changes. With my shift to self-hosting, it's essentially free to try new things and explore. I don't have to fret about spinning up another $5/mo VPS I might not totally utilize or worry about how many CPU cycles or function invocations I will have.  I can just explore without limits. The only cost is my time.

Maybe I'm just being dumb and blissfully ignorant.

The main risk with my philosophy is overpaying and underutilizing. But sometimes that is worth it. It just removes a whole host of decisions from my mind. That's valuable to me. Hard to calculate that. I probably paid more for the maintenance plan on our van than I would out of pocket each time[^2], but I really like not having to swipe my credit card when we take it in for an oil change. Priceless. Looking at something like Railway or a paid Cloudflare plan, I'd probably be spending about $240/year for my apps. That's about how much I paid for my HP EliteDesk G6. So in a few months, I'll be saving money. I'm of course in a privileged position to be able to pay for some of these things upfront. 

But I do lose out on flexibility. I'm limited by hardware running in my closet. I can't just create more RAM out of thin air. Especially in this economy.

There's value in knowing how much things really cost though. It's an important factor of deciding if you can afford something, or if it's worth it. I would probably drive less if I thought "oh man this trip to Target is going to cost $30." Is going to Target worth that much? I've got no clue. But it would make me think twice. I pay my car insurance and taxes once a year. I fill up gas once or twice a month. By the time I'm deciding to drive, the money has been spent. Sunk cost. It's difficult for me to know if owning a car is worth it if I don't actually know how much it is costing me.

I started thinking about all this because I'm considering using the Claude API with OpenCode or something. Just to see. I've mostly avoided paying API pricing for things like Claude[^3]. I don't like the idea of knowing how much every dumb question or code change costs me. The same way my coworkers are not thinking about much that 5 minute call is or that meeting with 60 people is. My 2 options are to pay $20/month for a subscription, or some unknown amount with the API. I lean towards the $20/month. But why not try the API and see how much it is? Maybe I won't use it as much because I'm worried about how much each prompt is costing me. If I pay the flat $20/month, maybe I'd use it more. So far, I chosen neither and am instead worry about which is the right one. That is definitely the cheapest option. At least money wise. Sanity wise? TBD.

When this post began, it was going to be great argument for hiding the true costs of something from yourself to liberate and free your mind. I'm of course wrong. Well, maybe mostly wrong. Or just sometimes wrong. There is not a once size fits all answer. Sometimes you need to pay for flexibility. Sometimes you need to pay for peace of mind. I think I'll try the Claude API just to get an idea on how much things really cost...and then [multiply by 10](https://youtu.be/u7jO5slUiUw?si=JYg3mWtfL6dgZydp) or so to see what the costs will be when AI companies decide they need to make money.
