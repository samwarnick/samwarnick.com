I'm trying not to make every post about AI. But here we are. Since I started using agents at work, I've been feeling more overwhelmed. I've been working more. I've been trying to fit in prompts and changes while "paying attention" in meetings. Or starting multiple tasks. AI fatigue they call it. I feel it. I have a long list of things to do, and it's easy to think that with AI, I can get it all done. At the same time. Quickly. Some people might be able to, but not me. I'm not as good as an AI at context switching[^1]. I can do a single thing at a time. So I needed help to focus more.

Dave—a certified and professional Kanban evangelist[^2]—got me thinking that maybe I should try Kanban again. So with Obsidian and the [Kanban plugin](https://publish.obsidian.md/kanban/Obsidian+Kanban+Plugin), I setup a new board. Four columns—todo, waiting, in progress, and done. In progress can have a single task at a time. I loaded in all my existing tasks into the todo column and prioritized them. Work that is blocking my coworkers is typically higher priority than other work. When new work comes in, I have good visibility on how it stacks up to everything else I've got going on. At the end of the day, I archive all my done tasks and keep a log of what I did in my daily notes[^3].

So far, this has been working for me. I now rarely have more than one agent open at a time. I'm not bouncing between a bunch of things as Claude lets me know it's ready for some input from me. I'm only doing one task at a time, but with AI, those individual tasks go faster. It's a reminder to me that AI is really just a tool. AI is not all the sudden unlocking parts of my brain and turning me into a super human[^4]. I still need to give my brain the room and rest it needs to work effectively. For me, I can handle about one thing at a time. You might be different. Good for you if you are.
