I've never been one to think much about work outside of work hours. Generally, I forget who I work for and what I do. But lately, I've been thinking more about work. It coincided with increased usage of AI at work. My initial thought was that using AI was somehow making me think more about work. A sneaky conspiracy between my boss and AI companies. While I still think AI is partially to blame, it's not the whole story.

I don't particularly like doing product work. Reading through requirements, fixing bugs, building a new modal with a form in it. I like when developers and engineers are my customers, not actual customers or stakeholders. I sometimes enjoy thinking about code more than actually writing it. Instead of building the modal, I want to build the modal component everyone else uses to build their modals. I want to help everyone else have the tools they need to do their jobs. That's the level I enjoy working at.

I've been doing more of that work lately. Design system work. Helping create a generator and packages for new backend TypeScript services. Creating proofs of concepts for workflow orchestration and other patterns. This is the fun stuff to me. I think that is the main reason I've been thinking more about work outside of work. For better or worse, I'm working on the types of problems that stick in my brain. It's been a deliberate move to focus more on these things this year, and it just happened to coincide with more AI usage.

I would still prefer to not think about work when I'm not working, but I am glad I've had a long streak of working on things I enjoy.
