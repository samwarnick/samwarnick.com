Carter posed the question: You have been given one month away from your obligations to use your talents to enact societal good. What do you go do?

I have a pretty boring answer. I only really have one talent. Hard for me to admit, but I think I am above average when it comes to programming. I think I would like to help kids and teens learn to build websites, and also help local small business owners with their websites.

With kids, I think it's mostly about exposure to programming than really teaching them. It took me a few starts to really get into programming, but I still remember how cool it felt the first time I got HTML I wrote to show up in a browser as a kid. Would be cool to help some kids have that experience and hopefully spark something. I'd make them do it the old fashioned way—without AI.

I respect small business owners. It is hard work, and they are vital to our communities and economy. Most do not have good websites, but could really benefit from having a great one. I could help with that. Better yet, I'd have the kids do it. I probably wouldn't help just any business. I'd develop some set of criteria and have an application, like they were applying for a grant or something. Need to balance the requirements so I wouldn't shut out people who could really use it but just don't have time to apply. I would probably extend small businesses to include local charities as well. But I think that would be a nice way to help out the local community. Or I could help them figure out what software and hardware would make their lives easier.

Maybe there are better things I could do, but this sounds interesting to me.
