I've always been pretty interested in bookbinding and publishing. As a kid, I'd make little notebooks and journals fairly often. One of my favorite projects in school was making and binding a book in the 5th grade. We researched an endangered animal in science, drew pictures in art, and write stories in English. Then, with special thread from Germany, we put it all together. During the pandemic, my daughter started to show some interest in making books. We started with what we had—printer paper, cardstock, and cereal boxes. Then we upgraded to a couple kits from [Hollander's](https://hollanders.com). Before I knew it, I had set up a workbench in my office, got a bunch of tools, and made quite a few books and journals as gifts for friends and family. It's been a while since I've made anything, but I still follow a couple bookbinding related channels on YouTube.

One of the most impressive channels is [Four Keys Book Arts](https://www.youtube.com/@FourKeysBookArts). Dennis is a talented self-taught bookbinder. His projects are truly art. He marbles his own paper. He creates his own tools to work leather. He makes his own end bands. He's always doing something new. He makes mistakes and finds creative solutions to fix them. It is all incredibly impressive and inspiring.

His latest endeavor might be tough to beat. Andy Weir, the author of _The Martian_, _Artemis_, and _Project Hail Mary_, asked Dennis to re-bind his books for him. Full control. Dennis went all out. You need to just watch what he created.
